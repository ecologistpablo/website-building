## Go deeper (open access)

These quests are a short path; the field already publishes free courses, scripts, and books. We paraphrase their goals below—follow the links for full material.

### Core R & reproducible workflows

- **[The Carpentries — Programming with R](https://swcarpentry.github.io/r-novice-inflammation/)** teaches modular analysis with loops and functions using worked examples—not every R function, but habits that match our Base R quests ([Software Carpentry, CC BY](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.27353)).
- **[R for Reproducible Scientific Analysis](https://swcarpentry.github.io/r-novice-gapminder/)** (Carpentries) extends that to multi-step, reproducible pipelines—close to how we chain dplyr and save scripts in RStudio.
- **[R for Data Science (2e)](https://r4ds.hadley.nz/)** (Wickham, Çetinkaya-Rundel, Grolemund) is the standard free book for import → tidy → transform → plot; our dplyr track follows the same verb order ([CC BY-NC-ND](https://r4ds.hadley.nz/)).
- **[Advanced R](https://adv-r.hadley.nz/)** (Wickham) explains why R behaves like a functional language and how `map`-style thinking fits data analysis—background for our purrr track.

### Marine & ecological statistics (people & labs)

- **[Seascape Models — Chris Brown](https://www.seascapemodels.org/)** (University of Tasmania) shares open teaching on predictive ecological modelling in R, with course notes and data on [GitHub](https://github.com/cbrown5/predictive-ecological-models)—quantitative marine decision-making with reproducible scripts.
- **[Statistics for ecologists (Master course)](https://github.com/oliviergimenez/statistics-for-ecologists-Master-courses)** (Gimenez) — slides, practicals, and R code under [CC BY 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/); strong on GLMMs and Bayesian ideas used in capture–recapture and population ecology.
- **[Bayesian Data Analysis in Ecology](https://tobiasroth.github.io/BDAEcology/)** (Korner-Nievergelt et al.) — free, evolving e-book with R/Stan examples; useful once regression and `purrr`-style replication make sense.
- **[R Workshops @ UQ](https://mathmarecol.github.io/RWorkshop/)** — long-running workshops from mathematical ecologists at the University of Queensland on R and applied modelling for researchers ([SCIE3360](https://course-profiles.uq.edu.au/course-profiles/SCIE3360-22218-7620) also stresses reproducible data science in R for life and environmental sciences).

### Universities & field training (context, not enrollment)

- **University of St Andrews** [Statistical Ecology MSc](https://www.st-andrews.ac.uk/subjects/statistics/statistical-ecology-msc/) and [incoming MSc guidance](https://www.st-andrews.ac.uk/mathematics-statistics/students/incoming-msc-students/) treat R as assumed tooling for modern applied statistics; CREEM runs structured stats training for St Andrews researchers.
- **University of Plymouth** embeds statistics workshops in marine biology field courses and points students to R-focused texts via [library guides](https://plymouth.libguides.com/marinebiology) (e.g. biologist-oriented R and reproducible research titles).
- **Max Planck Institute of Animal Behavior — [AniMove](https://animove.org/)** runs open-source–focused training on movement, remote sensing, and R; recorded material is linked from [Movebank teaching](https://www.movebank.org/cms/movebank-content/teaching-and-training).
- **FIU** — Simon Dedman’s open [graduate stats / R materials](https://github.com/SimonDedman/statscourse) cover tidying, SDMs, and causal modelling on marine datasets (see also reproducible reef analyses on [Zenodo](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19835079)).
- **Galápagos** — the [Charles Darwin Foundation](https://www.darwinfoundation.org/en/news/all-news-stories/charles-darwin-foundation-and-the-ecuadorian-society-for-statistics-provide-free-statistics-and-r-programming-training-courses-to-the-community-of-puerto-ayora-galapagos/) has run free community courses on statistics and R with local partners; the [Galapagos Science Center](https://www.galapagosscience.org/) supports interdisciplinary, data-heavy conservation research.

### Functional tools (packages & references)

- **[purrr](https://purrr.tidyverse.org/)** (Henry & Wickham) — official reference for typed `map` functions used in our Intermediate purrr track.
- **[data.table](https://rdatatable.gitlab.io/data.table/)** — documentation and vignettes for `[i, j, by]` syntax in our Advanced track.
- **[Big Book of R](https://bigbookofr.com/)** — curated chapters linking base R, tidyverse, and workflow topics; handy index when you outgrow quests.

### Self-paced ecology coding

- **[Coding Club — Data Science for Ecologists](https://ourcodingclub.github.io/course.html)** (University of Edinburgh) — free, self-paced R tutorials and quizzes aimed at environmental scientists; good parallel practice alongside any quest level.

*If you use AI tools while learning, treat these sources as the ground truth: run their code, compare output to ours, and cite them when you adapt their workflows.*
