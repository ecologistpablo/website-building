## Go deeper (open access)

Computer-science habits for ecologists are taught openly elsewhere too. Below are credible, mostly free resources this crash course echoes—paraphrased, not copied.

### Programming & reproducibility

- **[The Carpentries — Programming with R](https://swcarpentry.github.io/r-novice-inflammation/)** emphasises language-agnostic ideas—automation, functions, and modular scripts—using small datasets; aligns with our types, functions, and debugging quests ([DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.27353](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.27353)).
- **[R for Reproducible Scientific Analysis](https://swcarpentry.github.io/r-novice-gapminder/)** (Carpentries) walks through building an analysis that someone else can re-run—companion to our reproducibility and `set.seed()` quests.
- **[Good enough practices in scientific computing](https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.00037)** (Wilson et al.) — short paper on data management, versioning, and workflow; same principles as validate-inputs and plan-before-code quests.
- **[Reproducible Research in R for Ocean Biosciences (RRROBOTS)](https://github.com/Open-Data-Science-at-SIO/RRROBOTS)** — seminar materials on version control, documentation, and replicating published figures (Scripps / ocean biosciences audience, widely transferable).

### Open data science & universities

- **[Open Data Science Training at UQ](http://ohi-science.org/uq-training/)** (Lowndes et al.; see also [Lowndes *et al.* 2017, *Nature Ecology & Evolution*](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41559-017-0160)) — workshop model for R, RStudio, Git, GitHub, and R Markdown as a reproducible stack; matches our files/paths and version-control mindset quests.
- **[R Workshops @ UQ](https://mathmarecol.github.io/RWorkshop/)** — applied R for ecologists; useful after CS quests when you move into statistics-heavy work.
- **University of Florida [FOR 6934 Reproducible Quantitative Methods](https://ffgs.ifas.ufl.edu/)** (syllabus public online) — graduate course on tidy data, Git/GitHub, R Markdown, and databases in natural resources; parallels our composition, validation, and documentation quests.
- **University of St Andrews** [incoming MSc notes](https://www.st-andrews.ac.uk/mathematics-statistics/students/incoming-msc-students/) recommend R and RStudio before statistical ecology modules—same “learn the tool before the model” ordering we use here.

### Books & references (free online)

- **[R for Data Science (2e)](https://r4ds.hadley.nz/)** — workflow, project structure, and literate programming ([CC BY-NC-ND](https://r4ds.hadley.nz/)).
- **[Advanced R](https://adv-r.hadley.nz/)** — deeper look at objects, environments, and functions once base types and assignment click.
- **Plymouth [Marine Science library guides](https://plymouth.libguides.com/marine)** list standard texts on R for biologists and reproducible research (e.g. *Reproducible Research with R and RStudio*)—worth knowing titles when you need a library copy.

### Conservation & field communities

- **[Charles Darwin Foundation — Galápagos R training](https://www.darwinfoundation.org/en/news/all-news-stories/charles-darwin-foundation-and-the-ecuadorian-society-for-statistics-provide-free-statistics-and-r-programming-training-courses-to-the-community-of-puerto-ayora-galapagos/)** — community workshops on statistics and R for conservation and local institutions.
- **[Chris Brown / Seascape Models](https://www.seascapemodels.org/)** — open course materials show how marine scientists document and share R workflows in practice.

*Cite these resources when you reuse their code or teaching ideas; our quests are a ladder, not a replacement for their depth.*
